TOURISM

Tourism is one of the main industries of Baguio City. That was a result of the climate and history of the area. The city's population increased by four times over the breaks in the winter and summer. In 2011 Baguio City win Trip Advisor's title for Traveler's Choice Destination in Asia.

Baguio is the tourism center of the Cordillera, serving as a gateway to other northern tourist spots such as Sagada, Mount Pulag, and Banaue. Indeed, its tourist campaigns emphasize surrounding sites and points of interest, such as La Trinidad.



Appetizing Food and Souvenirs

Baguio City was named the Traveller's Choice Destination Award (Asia category) by TripAdvisor in 2011 as a top tourist destination in the Philippines. It outperformed twenty-five other Asian locations. Burnham Park, Mines View Park, Teacher's Camp, The Mansion, and Baguio Cathedral are five of the top Baguio tourist attractions.

Furthermore, aside from strawberries, veggies, and strawberry-flavored taho on the side, travelers frequently return home with a take-home treats. Good Shepherd goods like strawberry jam, peanut brittle, ube jam, and sesame crunch are popular. Non-food objects include bulul and the iconic barrel man, as well as woven things such as local backpacks and baskets.

Panagbenga Festival and Places That You Might Like:

The annual Panagbenga Festival acknowledges the local floral industry as a vital contributor to the city's economy. Panagbenga, however, has a deeper meaning for the Ibagiws: it was designed to motivate people to rise against the destruction caused by the 1990 Luzon earthquake.

Every February, the event involves a parade of brightly colored floats covered in flowers, as well as street dance performances by dancers costumed in flower-inspired costumes. Dance is a cultural component. There are more than 80 hotels and inns available to house these travelers such as Cathedral, Bell Church, Night Market, Teacher Camp, Baguio Museum, Mines View, White House or Hunted Mansion, Burnham Park, Botanical Garden, Stone Kingdom, and The Mansion.
