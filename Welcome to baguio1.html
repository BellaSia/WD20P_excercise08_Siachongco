THE SUMMER CAPITAL OF THE PHILIPPINES

The province of Benguet in Northern Luzon is home to the first-class and highly urbanized metropolis of Baguio, also known as the City of Baguio. From 1901 until 1916, Baguio served as the provincial capital of Benguet before becoming a chartered city. Despite being formally separated from Benguet, Baguio continues to serve as the province's and the whole Cordillera region's economic hub. It is regarded as the place where nearby areas merge. Baguio attracts visitors from other provinces and nations because of its cool temperature, history, culture, and locally produced goods. Particularly the Manilenos go there to get away from the heat, noise, crowds, and general chaos of the city. The location is ideal for a highland hideaway in the Grand Canyon.
